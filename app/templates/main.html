• When scholars suffer attacks for their teaching, research, publications or public talks, other scholars begin to self-censor their work in fear of being victims of similar attacks. This problem of self-censorship leads to reduced freedom to think, question, and share ideas for everyone.
  • By filling out this survey, we at Scholars at Risk hope to quantify self-censorship to prove that it is a issue prevalent across the globe.
